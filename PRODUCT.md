# Product

## Register

product

## Users

React developers evaluating, integrating, and maintaining a component library, plus visitors using the documentation site to compare components and learn their APIs. They need familiar controls that remain readable and predictable while accurately demonstrating Apple-style Liquid Glass.

## Product Purpose

Provide an accessible React component library with real environment-aware refraction, highlights, depth, and graceful browser fallbacks. Success means the components remain recognizably glass on white, dark, and image backgrounds without becoming opaque panels or losing interaction clarity.

## Brand Personality

Precise, translucent, restrained, and tactile. The interface should feel engineered rather than decorated, with visual depth that responds to its surroundings.

## Anti-references

- Glass that disappears on white backgrounds.
- Opaque white panels presented as glass.
- Generic blur-only cards without refraction, edge light, or depth.
- Components whose material, spacing, or interaction states drift from the shared system.
- Decorative effects that reduce text contrast or obscure standard controls.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve real refraction and environmental transparency in every glass surface.
2. Keep the material legible across light, dark, and image backgrounds.
3. Use one coherent material system across components and the documentation site.
4. Demonstrate the library with its own components wherever practical.
5. Treat keyboard access, focus visibility, ARIA, reduced motion, and browser fallback as core behavior.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic ARIA, reduced-motion and reduced-transparency preferences, high-contrast modes, SSR-safe rendering, and readable text and controls across all supported backgrounds.
